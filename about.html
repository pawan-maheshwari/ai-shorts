About AIShorts

A fast, broadsheet-styled digest of the AI stories that actually matter — the day's models, money, policy, research, safety, robotics and infrastructure, in a clean read you can finish in a few minutes.

What we do

AIShorts gathers the most significant artificial-intelligence headlines from across the web and presents them as a swipeable, newspaper-style front page. Each card gives you the headline, a concise summary, the source, and a link to read the full story. An on-device assistant can summarise the day, pull together what happened at a given company, and compare the running stories — all from the news currently loaded, with no tracking and no account required.

Why it exists

The pace of AI news is relentless and most of it is noise. AIShorts is built for busy readers who want signal: a calm, editorial layout instead of an endless feed, and just enough context to decide what's worth your time.

How it's made

AIShorts is an independent project. Headlines are compiled from public news sources and feed providers; summaries are written to be brief and neutral. The Site is supported by advertising — see our Privacy Policy for how cookies and ads work.

Who runs it

AIShorts is created and moderated by Pawan Maheshwari, an AI transformation lead with two decades in technology and service delivery. It is part of a wider set of educational AI projects, including AI University.
